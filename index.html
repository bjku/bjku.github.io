Understanding the Cube

At first, the cube can be a little daunting, because when it's mixed up, it just looks like a bunch of colors on a 3x3x3 cube. So the first step we want to take doesn't involve any algorithms, just thinking about how the cube works.

The cube has six faces, each facing having a color. These center cubes don't really move, it's all of the other cubelets that move around them.

For each color, there's four edges, and each of these edges are shared with another face's color.

Likewise, for each color, there's four corner, and each of these corners are shared with TWO other face's colors.

Pretty much every move you're gonna do involves moving edges and corners around center faces. Play around with the cube on the right so you get a good feel for how the cube works.

First Layer

After the cube is totally mixed up, the first step is to pick a color. We can think of the cube like it has three layers, and we start solving it from the bottom up. First, we solve the first/bottom layer, followed by the second/middle layer, and finally comes the third/last layer. Most beginners are recommended to start with the white face, and that's what we'll be using in our examples with the cube on the right.

So using white as our starting color, we want to build a cross on the white face, while also lining up the red, blue, orange, and green edges with their center. There's not really any algorithms here, because there's nothing solved yet to mess up.

Finishing the first layer

After getting the cross down, the next step is to solve the corners of the first layer. So, one at a time, you'll look for the white corners. When you find one, if it's in the third layer, then you want to move it above the corner where it should be. Now, there's three different scenarios, depending on which way the white sticker is facing. Here I'll be using the orange blue corner as an example.

1. It's facing left

2. It's facing right

3. It's facing up

Solving the Middle Layer

The next step is to solve the middle layer, which involves finding the four edge pieces with no yellow or white, and placing them in the correct spot. There's four possible scenarious for any given middle edge, and I'll be using the orange and blue edge as an example.

1. It's already in the right place in the right orientation. Yay!

2. It's in the top layer, and the middle layer sticker it connects to is on the left.

3. It's in the top layer, and the middle layer sticker it connects to is on the right.

4. It's already in the right place, but the orientation is wrong. :(

Starting the Top Layer

The cube is starting to look mostly solved huh? After completing the first two layers, the next step is to make a cross on the yellow side, like we did in the beginning, except there's algorithms this time. Ignoring the corners, the top can look like one of three things:

1. Like a V

2. Like an I

3. Like a period

Making a Good Cross

So now we have a cross, but it may or may not be a good cross like the one we made in the first layer. So we gotta fix that. If it's not a good cross, there's only one scenario you'll have to look for:

1. Two edges are the right color, and adjacent edges are swapped

Placing the Corners

Now that our yellow cross is perfect, we have to get these corners right. The first step to accomplishing that is to make sure the corners are in the right spot. We'll only need one algorithm that cycles three of the corners' positions counter-clockwise, while one stays in the same spot. If none of the corners are in the right spot, then just do the algorithm anywhere, and then one corner will be in the right spot.

1. Corners need to move counter-clockwise, takes one iteration

2. Corners need to move counter-clockwise, takes two iterations

3. No corners are in the right spot, takes two or three iterations

Solid Top Layer

We're almost done! The corners are in the right spot, so now we're on step away from solving the cube. To do that we'll have to flip the corners so that the yellow sticker is facing up. Here, we're going to hold the cube sideways, and repeat a four turn motion until a corner is solved, and then move another unsolved corner in, repeating the process until all corners are turned up.
